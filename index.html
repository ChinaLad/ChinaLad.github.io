Projects

Why?

For me, programming projects are more than just code—they’re a way to solve problems, satisfy my curiosity, and constantly challenge myself. I love the instant feedback programming provides. It’s not just about writing lines of code; it's about seeing the direct impact of your efforts and having something tangible to show for it.

My projects span a wide range of topics—from simulations and games to apps and machine learning. I don’t limit myself to a specific area; instead, I explore whatever piques my interest. Sometimes, I’ll watch a video on a topic or stumble upon some technology I don’t fully understand, and that curiosity drives me to learn more and see if I can create something similar.

The process of building these projects is where I really grow. Each one helps me develop more complex code, understand research papers, or learn from someone else’s work. And with just two weeks of effort, I can create something unique and valuable. When you think about it, by the end of a year, that could mean up to 27 distinct projects—each one teaching me something new.

Working on a project can be intimidating at first, especially when there are so many possible solutions. It’s not like solving a straightforward Leetcode problem; it’s about navigating the uncertainty and gradually building something cool. One project that stands out for me is a natural selection simulation I created. I built a virtual world where simple creatures with random brain connections evolved over generations. It was fascinating to see how these virtual creatures adapted and became better at specific tasks over time.

In the end, these projects are more than just practice—they’re a way for me to push boundaries, learn continuously, and create something that reflects my growth and passion for technology.
